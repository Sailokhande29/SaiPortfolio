My Projects

Hand Gesture Car with Arduino

A hand gesture-controlled car that I have developed using Arduino typically involves sensors like an accelerometer (e.g., MPU6050) to detect hand movements. These sensors are interfaced with the Arduino, which processes the data and translates specific gestures into car commands such as forward, backward, left, or right. The car's motors are controlled via a motor driver like the L298N, and wireless communication is achieved through NRF24L01 modules, transmitting the gesture data to the car. This project combines sensor technology, motor control, and wireless communication to create an interactive and fun robotic experience.

Brain Impact

This project focuses on designing protective headgear to prevent brain injuries, especially concussions, in athletes. By analyzing concussion data and studying the brain's regions, the project involves building models that simulate head collisions. A brain model equipped with impact sensors is used to test the effectiveness of different materials in reducing concussion severity. The aim is to create headgear that can significantly mitigate brain injury during sports activities, while also considering ongoing research in the field for improved safety measures.
